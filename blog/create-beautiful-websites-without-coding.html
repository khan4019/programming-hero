Create Beautiful Websites Without Coding!

  • By Jhankar Mahbub

  • 24 Feb 2021

We are slowly moving towards the modem world and technologies are evolving more than ever! Creating a simple website, or a full-fledged web application requires you to hire a web developer that might be out of your budget! Donâ€™t worry, technologies have got your back, you can create amazing websites and web apps without writing a single line of code. Just drag and drop, and your website is ready.



But, What is a CMS(Content Management System)?

A content management system (CMS) is a software platform that enables users to add, publish, modify, and manage website content without requiring users to have specialized programming knowledge.

Below is the list of some top and powerful Web CMS technologies that you should keep an eye on!

WordPress - https://wordpress.org/



WordPress is an open-source software you can use to create a beautiful website, blog, or app. WordPress.org was launched in 2003 and has simplified website development for everyone. It started as a blogging platform and has now grown into a full-blown CMS platform to the extent that 65% of the active websites now use WordPress.
While WordPress offers a variety of free themes, you can also buy third-party themes. WordPress lets you use plugins to help you extend its feature set and integrate different applications with the website to automate the workflow. Wordpress is a perfect choice for not only static or blogging sites but also for full-fledged web applications.

Joomla - https://www.joomla.org/



Joomla! is an award-winning content management system (CMS), which enables you to build web sites and powerful online applications. It is yet another powerful and most used Web CMS. Over 2 million websites use Joomla as its CMS. Joomla is a powerful CMS if your content strategy involves a diverse content mix. The CMS is mobile-friendly, supports multilingual websites, provides multi-level user permissions, and can provide a thorough set of features without the need for third-party integrations/extensions.

Wix - https://www.wix.com/



Design and build your own high-quality websites. Whether youâ€™re promoting your business, showcasing your work, opening your store, or starting a blogâ€”you can do it all with the Wix website builder. Wix is the answer. You can either build your website from the ground up or pick a design from the repository of 500 readymade theme templates. One of the strongest suits of Wix is its range of features that make it easy for different types of businesses to set up a website.
Not only Web CMS, but Wix also provides features such as Logo makers and much more that makes Wix a top choice for your business.

Shopify - https://www.shopify.in/



While you can create amazing static, dynamic, e-commerce websites using other CMS, Shopify is known for its E-commerce CMS and site development. It is one of the top choices to develop and start e-commerce sites. While Shopify can be integrated with WordPress and other platforms, it is also available as a standalone CMS for e-commerce websites. Its expansive capabilities help store owners right from setting up their store to promoting their offerings across various ad channels.
You can set up a full-fledged blog, tackle cart abandonment issues, integrate social media platforms to sell on Facebook, manage inventory, optimize the website from the SEO perspective, and integrate Google Analytics to track the storeâ€™s performance.

Squarespace - https://www.squarespace.com/



Squarespace is a modern and young web CMS. Squarespace is known for its intuitive and ridiculously simple drag-and-drop website builder that lets you build an aesthetic website interface. The CMS is used primarily by SMBs, entrepreneurs, musicians, restaurants, and bloggers. Squarespace acts as a one-stop-shop for users by providing all essential website ingredients such as domain name, web hosting, SSL certificate, extensions, etc. right from its basic pricing plans.
Squarespace comes with a built-in repository of mobile-friendly themes that you can modify using CSS. While Squarespace is an excellent choice for smaller websites, it may not suit your requirements as you scale your content efforts in the longer run.

Thus there are many various CMS available, you should choose one that fits your requirement and your business.
